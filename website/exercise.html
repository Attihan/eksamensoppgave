P35's name is Ellen Gleditschs hus. She was a pioneer in radioactivity and radiochemistry. She discovered a more accurate determination of the half-life of radium. This had significant importance for international science. She studied in Copenhagen and was a research assistant for Marie Curie. Gleditsch was a prominent advocate for women's rights and Norway's second female professor."
P50's name is Katti Anker Møllers hus. She was particularly known for her advocacy for contraception, sexual education, and children's rights. According to her perspective, public responsibility and just laws were crucial to ensure the rights of both children and mothers. Møller was an active proponent of the Castberg child laws, which protected children born out of wedlock. In 1924, she founded the Mother Hygiene Office in collaboration with women in the labor movement.
